Compliance Checklist

ICO Voice AI Compliance Checklist: 18 Points.

Actionable checklist for UK businesses deploying AI voice systems. Based on ICO guidance and UK GDPR requirements.

How to Use This Checklist.

Work through each section before deploying an AI voice system. Revisit annually or when you change providers. Each item includes what is required and why it matters.

Items marked with “Legal requirement” are obligations under UK GDPR. Items marked with “Recommended” are strong good practice that demonstrates due diligence.

Before You Deploy.

1. Identify your lawful basis for processing

Legal requirement — Determine whether legitimate interests, consent, or contractual necessity applies to your use of AI call handling. Document your reasoning in writing. For most businesses, legitimate interests is appropriate for answering calls; consent may be needed for recording.

Reference: UK GDPR Article 6

2. Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment

Legal requirement (where applicable) — Required when deploying new technology that processes personal data at scale. Document the data you will process, the risks to callers, and the mitigations you have in place. Even when not strictly mandatory, a DPIA demonstrates due diligence.

Reference: UK GDPR Article 35, ICO DPIA guidance

3. Review your current privacy policy

Legal requirement — Check that your privacy policy covers AI-assisted call handling, voice data processing, and third-party processors. Update it before going live with your AI system.

4. Appoint or consult a Data Protection Officer

Recommended — Not legally required for all organisations, but strongly recommended when deploying AI systems that process voice data. Consider external DPO services if you do not have one in-house. A DPO can review your DPIA and advise on compliance gaps.

Privacy and Transparency.

5. Add AI disclosure to your call flow

Legal requirement — Callers must know they are interacting with AI. Include a clear notification at the start of each call. This is not optional — transparency is a core GDPR principle.

Reference: UK GDPR Articles 13 & 14

6. Provide call recording notification

Legal requirement (if recording) — If you record calls, inform callers before recording begins and state the purpose. Offer an opt-out where practical. The notification should be clear and unambiguous.

7. Update your website privacy policy

Legal requirement — Add sections covering: what voice data you collect, why you collect it, how long you keep it, who processes it, and what rights callers have. Name your AI provider by category (e.g. “AI voice processing provider”) at minimum.

8. Make your privacy notice accessible

Recommended — Provide a way for callers to access your full privacy policy. Mention your website URL during the call or include it in follow-up communications such as appointment confirmations.

9. Document your transparency measures

Recommended — Keep a record of how and when callers are informed about AI processing. This demonstrates compliance if the ICO investigates. Include the exact wording of your call notifications.

Security and Technical Measures.

10. Verify your provider’s security certifications

Recommended — Check for ISO 27001, SOC 2, or equivalent certifications. Ask about encryption (in transit and at rest), access controls, and incident response procedures. Your provider’s security directly affects your compliance.

11. Configure data retention periods

Legal requirement — Set retention periods proportionate to your stated purpose. The ICO expects you to justify your chosen timeframe. Six months is a common baseline for call recordings, but your needs may differ. Document your reasoning.

Reference: UK GDPR Article 5(1)(e)

12. Enable access controls

Recommended — Restrict who in your organisation can access call recordings, transcripts, and caller data. Use role-based access where available. Not everyone needs access to everything.

13. Establish a data breach response plan

Legal requirement — Know what to do if caller data is compromised. The ICO must be notified within 72 hours of becoming aware of a qualifying breach. Have a documented response plan that includes your AI provider’s role in breach notification.

Reference: UK GDPR Article 33

14. Test data deletion processes

Recommended — Verify that data is actually deleted when retention periods expire. Test the process before going live, not after. Ask your provider to demonstrate their deletion procedures.

Third-Party Processor Obligations.

15. Sign a Data Processor Agreement

Legal requirement — Required by UK GDPR Article 28. Your DPA must cover: processing scope, security obligations, sub-processor approval, data return or deletion on termination, and your right to audit. Do not deploy an AI voice system without a signed DPA.

Reference: UK GDPR Article 28

16. Map your data flows

Recommended — Document where caller data goes: your AI provider, any sub-processors, cloud hosting locations, CRM integrations. Check that each transfer has appropriate safeguards. If data leaves the UK, ensure Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decisions are in place.

Ongoing Compliance.

17. Establish a process for data subject requests

Legal requirement — Callers can request access to their data, ask for deletion, or object to processing. You must respond within one month. Have a documented process ready, including how you will retrieve data from your AI provider.

Reference: UK GDPR Articles 15–22

18. Schedule annual compliance reviews

Recommended — Regulations change, ICO guidance is updated, and your business evolves. Review your DPIA, privacy notices, retention periods, and processor agreements at least annually. Update as needed and document any changes.

Frequently Asked Questions.

Is this checklist based on official ICO guidance?

This checklist is based on the ICO’s published guidance on AI and data protection, the UK GDPR text, and the ICO’s AI and Data Protection Risk Toolkit. It is not a substitute for professional legal advice.

Do I need to complete every item on this checklist?

The items covering lawful basis, transparency, DPA, and data subject rights are legal requirements under UK GDPR. Others, such as DPO appointment for smaller organisations, are strong recommendations. All 18 points represent good practice for any business deploying AI voice systems.

How often should I review my compliance?

At minimum annually, or whenever you change AI providers, expand your use of AI, or when new ICO guidance is published. Regulations and enforcement priorities evolve, so regular reviews are essential.

What if my AI provider will not sign a DPA?

Consider a different provider. A Data Processor Agreement is a legal requirement under Article 28 of UK GDPR. Any reputable AI provider will have one ready to sign.

Can I use this checklist as evidence of compliance?

A completed checklist demonstrates due diligence but is not sufficient alone. You need the underlying documentation — DPIA, privacy notices, DPA, processing records — to support it. The checklist helps you identify what documentation you need.

Does this checklist apply to AI chat widgets as well as voice?

Yes. The same GDPR principles apply to any AI system processing personal data, whether voice-based or text-based. If your AI chat widget collects names, email addresses, or other personal data, these requirements apply.

This checklist provides general guidance based on publicly available ICO resources and UK GDPR requirements. It is not legal advice. For specific compliance guidance, consult a qualified data protection officer or solicitor. Regulations and ICO guidance may change — verify current requirements before making compliance decisions.
