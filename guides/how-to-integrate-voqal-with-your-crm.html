How to integrate Voqal AI with your CRM.

Last updated: 7 April 2026

Connect your AI receptionist to HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho CRM, or Pipedrive in under 15 minutes. Every call creates contacts, logs transcripts, and updates your pipeline — automatically.

Why integrate your CRM?

Without CRM integration, call data lives in one system and customer records in another. Your team ends up manually entering contact details, copying call notes, and updating deal stages — work that should happen automatically.

When Voqal AI is connected to your CRM, every call automatically creates or updates a contact record, attaches the full transcript, logs the outcome, and moves deals through your pipeline. No manual data entry, no missed follow-ups.

Step 1

Confirm your plan includes CRM integration.

CRM integration is available on the Pro (£397/mo) and Premium (£697/mo) plans. If you are currently on the Starter plan, you will need to upgrade to Pro to unlock CRM syncing.

Upgrading takes effect immediately — no downtime, no reconfiguration needed. Your existing AI receptionist setup carries over.

Step 2

Authorise the CRM connection.

Select your CRM from the integrations panel and click Connect. You will be redirected to your CRM's sign-in page to grant Voqal AI the necessary permissions.

Permissions requested:

  • • Create contacts — new callers become CRM records automatically
  • • Update contacts — returning callers get their record updated
  • • Log activities — call transcripts and outcomes attached to records
  • • Read pipelines — Voqal AI knows which stage deals are in

This uses secure OAuth authentication. Your CRM password is never shared with Voqal AI.

Step 3

Configure field mapping.

Map the data Voqal AI captures during calls to the correct fields in your CRM. Default mappings are applied automatically — you only need to adjust these if you use custom fields.

Default field mappings:

Caller name → Contact name

Phone number → Phone

Email → Email

Call transcript → Activity note

Call outcome → Activity type

Enquiry type → Lead source / tag

If your CRM uses custom fields (e.g., "Treatment Type" for dental practices or "Case Area" for law firms), map those here so the AI populates them directly.

Step 4

Set automation rules.

Define what happens in your CRM after each type of call. Rules run automatically on every interaction — no manual triggers needed.

Common automation rules:

  • • New caller → Create contact + create deal in "New Lead" stage
  • • Returning caller → Update existing contact + log call activity
  • • Appointment booked → Create task for staff + move deal to "Booked" stage
  • • Message taken → Create follow-up task assigned to relevant team member
  • • Missed call → Create high-priority task for callback
Step 5

Test and verify.

Make a test call and check that the data flows correctly into your CRM.

Verification checklist:

  • • Contact record created with correct name, phone, and email
  • • Call transcript attached as an activity or note
  • • Deal created in the correct pipeline stage
  • • Automation rules fired (tasks created, stages updated)
  • • Second test call updates the same contact (no duplicates)

If anything looks wrong, field mappings and rules can be adjusted instantly. Changes take effect on the next call — no restart required.
