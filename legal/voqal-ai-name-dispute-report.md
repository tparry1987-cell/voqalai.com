## 3. UK Trademark Law - Tom's Position

### 3a. Does a Canadian trademark have force in the UK?

**No.** Trademarks are strictly territorial. Canada's own IP office (CIPO) states: *"The protection of IP rights registered in Canada does not extend to the United Kingdom."* The Canadian company would need a **separate UK registration** to have enforceable rights in the UK. No evidence of any UK filing exists.

### 3b. Does registering "Voqal AI Ltd" at Companies House help?

**Marginally.** It:
- Creates a dated official record of UK incorporation under that name
- Blocks others from incorporating the identical name at Companies House
- Shows formal commitment

**But it does NOT give trademark rights.** Companies House registration and trademark registration are completely separate systems. It's a useful step but not a substitute for a trademark.

Cost: GBP 100 (digital), GBP 156 (same-day digital). Increased from GBP 50 on 1 Feb 2026.

### 3c. UK Trademark Registration - The Key Move

- Cost: **GBP 170** for one class online (rises to GBP 205 on 1 April 2026 -- file before then)
- GBP 50 per additional class
- Timeline: ~3-4 months (2-3 weeks examination, 2-month opposition window, 2 weeks registration)
- Gives exclusive rights to the mark for specified goods/services in the UK
- Right to stop others using a confusingly similar mark
- Even a pending application has a chilling effect

**Recommended classes for Tom:**
- Class 38 (telecommunications services)
- Class 42 (SaaS / software as a service)
- Class 35 (business services / answering service)

### 3d. Prior Use / Passing Off Rights

Even without a registered trademark, Tom has common law protection through **passing off** if he can prove:
1. **Goodwill** -- reputation among UK customers (invoices, revenue, website traffic, testimonials, marketing)
2. **Misrepresentation** -- the other party's use causes confusion among UK consumers
3. **Damage** -- actual or probable harm to Tom's goodwill

Also, **Section 11(3) Trade Marks Act 1994** provides a statutory defence: if Tom has been trading under the name before anyone else registers the trademark, his earlier right is protected.

### 3e. Nice Classification - Different Classes Help

The two businesses operate in fundamentally different markets. Identical trademarks **can legally coexist** in different classes (e.g., PENGUIN exists for books, biscuits, and clothing). Tom's AI voice receptionists for UK businesses vs. a mobile voice SDK for MENA developers = minimal confusion risk.

## 5. Answers to Tom's Specific Questions

### "If I register as a limited company today as Voqal AI, does that strengthen my position?"

**Yes, but only slightly.** It creates an official dated record and blocks the identical name at Companies House. The **real** protection comes from UK trademark registration (GBP 170). Do both.

### "Is there anything he can do legally to try and get me to stop using that name?"

**Realistically, no.** He would need to:
1. Have a UK trademark (he doesn't)
2. Or prove UK goodwill for passing off (he has none -- MENA-focused, pre-launch startup)
3. And even then, different products in different markets weakens any confusion argument

The only theoretical risk is if he files a UK trademark before Tom does. But Tom can oppose it based on prior use, and the different market/class argument still applies.

### "Can he fine me or sue me?"

**He cannot fine you** -- only courts/regulators can impose fines. He **could** sue you, but:
- He'd need to hire UK solicitors (expensive)
- He'd need a legal basis (he has none currently)
- Different country, different product, different market, no UK trademark = case would likely be dismissed
- Cost to him: GBP 50,000-500,000+ with very low chance of success
